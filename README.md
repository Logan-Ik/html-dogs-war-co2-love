The Wonderful World of Dogs 

A humorous, interactive website exploring the “impact” of dogs on the environment, energy, and society. Complete with playful facts, calculators, and a “Dog Army” enlistment page, this site is a mix of education and entertainment.

Website Pages

1. Home Page – “Why getting a dog helps the environment”

Explains fun “benefits” of dogs for the environment:

  Dogs help plant trees by digging holes
  Dogs produce natural fertilizer
  Dogs scare away “government CO₂-emitting birds”
  Adopting dogs reduces overpopulation
  Interactive dog power calculator:

  Calculates total power output based on number of dogs
  Dynamic table showing lightbulb watts vs. number of dogs
  Images for visual humor and engagement
  Link to learn more: *“Dogs can take over the world”*

2. CO₂ Impact Page – “How much CO₂ do dogs produce”**

Displays facts about the carbon pawprint of dogs:

  10–20kg dogs produce ~2.1kg CO₂/day
  ~770kg CO₂ per year on average
  Tips for reducing CO₂ output:

  Reduce overfeeding
  Choose sustainable dog food
  Reduce red meat, explore alternative proteins
  Interactive **CO₂ calculator**:

  Enter dog weight to estimate yearly CO₂ output

3. Dog Army Page – “Join the Dog Army Program!”

Shows playful “dog military models” with their special abilities:

  Beagle Bomber: detects explosives
  German Shepherd Guardian: protection and attack
  Labrador Loader: carries supplies
  Border Collie Commander: leads and strategizes
  Call to action to enroll your dog in the army
  Links back to other pages for full site navigation


Features

  Interactive calculators** for dog power and CO₂ footprint
  Tables with humorous facts** about dogs’ capabilities
  Multiple pages** linked together for smooth navigation
  Responsive layout** using simple CSS cards and tables
  Quirky and entertaining content for fun learning
  
How to Use

1. Open `index.html` in any modern browser.
2. Explore the pages using the links and navigation bar.
3. Use calculators:

   Enter the number of dogs to estimate total power output (watts)
   Enter dog weight to estimate yearly CO₂ impact
   
5. Navigate to the Dog Army page for extra fun content.
